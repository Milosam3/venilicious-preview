Cut from free-roaming venison on our Limpopo and Northern Cape hunting grounds, this is the leanest, most tender cut on the animal — the equivalent of a beef fillet, but wilder. Deep, clean flavour. No hormones, no feedlots, no shortcuts.

Field-dressed, dry-aged and vacuum-sealed within the cold chain, then delivered frozen-solid to your door. Best served rare to medium-rare; venison fillet is lean and turns quickly, so pull it early and let it rest.

  1. Thaw overnight in the fridge. Bring to room temperature 30 minutes before cooking.
  2. Pat dry, season generously with coarse salt. Get your coals or pan screaming hot.
  3. Sear 2–3 minutes per side for medium-rare (internal 52–55°C). Don't overcook — venison is lean.
  4. Rest 5–8 minutes under foil before slicing against the grain.
